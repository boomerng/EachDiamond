Quality Management

Each Diamond team consists of technology experts in super-abrasive industry, and we have been well recognized by the market. Here are our manufacture procedures:

  • Produce mono-crystalline and multi-crystalline diamond from graphite.
  • Produce cubic boron nitride (CBN) from hexagonal boron nitride (BN), mono-crystalline, multi-crystalline diamond micron powders and CBN micron powder.
  • Produce CVD diamond from methane.
  • Apply the coating of abrasives and micron powders of diamond and CBN.
  • Manufacture PDC cutters from diamond microns.

Although super-abrasive industry has many different products, its R&D and quality control principles and solutions are quite similar. They can summarized into three aspects:

  • First is material properties such as crystal structure and fracture mode.
  • Second is quality which includes impact resistance, abrasion resistance and thermal stability.
  • The last one is control target such as crystal shape, color, size, surface purity and more.

Each Diamond has established an effective quality management system. Each Diamond strives to provide our customers and partners the best quality PDC cutters.

We strictly follow our control guidelines on raw material inspection, its treatment, production process, second stage inspection, performance test and final evaluation. Our guidelines follow six sigma methodology.

Quality Control

Incoming Material
We only pick the quality diamonds with complete crystals with higher toughness and thermal stability to produce our diamond micron powder. The diamond micron powder we produce is top grade quality with equilateral shape and transparent crystal, standard size, narrow size distribution, PPM cleanness and non-magnetic.

The tungsten carbide we use comes from popular brands. They have coarse crystalline structure and with high toughness, excellent abrasion resistance, high bending strength, top thermal and corrosion resistance.

Research and Development
Here are the procedures we follow for our R&D: We identify product or service desired from customers, its classification of quality characteristics. Then, we design, and come up with a prototype. We do requirement testing, verification, validation and identify its failure mode. When we are in our pre-production phase, we send our samples to our partners for in-field testing. We have partners in different continents, which really helps the spread of the test results for further improvements. Only when we are satisfied with our results, we enter lot production.

Structure Inspection
The Optical Microscope Imaging Apparatus (OMIA) is used to precisely measure dimension of our products. The Scanning Electron Microscope (SEM) is used to probe its micron structure. The Scanning Acoustic Microscope (SAM) is used to look for the flaws, delamination and hollows in the products.

Thermal Stability Test
We perform differential scanning calorimetry - Thermogravimetric - differential thermal analyzer (DSC-DTA). When heating PDC cutters in the oxidizing atmosphere, we measure the initial oxidation temperature, mass loss rate and weight loss ratio to determine the degree of oxidation and heat resistance.

Wear Ratio
Wear Ratio (WR) is a form to measure PDC cutter's abrasion resistance. For PDC cutters ground by the wheel, WR is defined as the ratio of the standard wheel weight loss to the PDC cutter diamond layer weight loss. The higher the ratio, the higher the abrasion resistance. Heat WR is the abrasion resistance after PDC cutter passes through a heat treatment. It is used to determine the abrasion variation of PDC cutters.

Impact Strength
Impact Strength (IS) is the ability to resist impact fracture of PDC cutters. We let a steel ball free fall from a certain height and hit the central area of diamond layer surface. We repeat this until flaw or breakage appear on the cutter. We then sum up all impact powers from these trials as the impact strength of the PDC cutter.

In order to better simulate impact on PDC cutters during drilling, we also perform tests by hitting the side of the PDC cutter. According to probability statistic law, the impact points of the ball compose an outer circle line.

Application Test
Other than simulating the drilling process, Each Diamond has also developed a proprietary technology for actual application test. We install a drill machine with power measurement on a giant platform. We first construct a drill bit with our PDC cutters. We then use it to drill on a homogeneous crystal granite. During the process, the bit will undergo through heavy-duty friction abrasion, impact, erosion and thermal abrasion. With the collected data, we evaluate our PDC cutters' overall performance on durability and drilling rate, and validate if our PDC cutters are ready for field test.

Field Test
We have partners in different continents to help us on field tests. We truly appreciate their help and partnership. Our product has gone through extensive tests in different geological formations before we give final approval to our products.
