Quality Management

The skills and know how we have include: making mono-crystalline and multi-crystalline diamond from graphite, Cubic Nitride Boron (CBN) from hexagonal BN, mono-crystalline and multi-crystalline diamond micron powders, CBN micron powder; CVD diamond from methane; the coating of abrasives and micron powders of diamond and CBN; and making PDC Cutters from diamond microns.

Quality Control

Incoming Material
The diamonds we use to make the diamond micron powder are complete crystals with higher toughness and thermal stability. The diamond micron powders are of top grade quality with equilateral shape and transparent crystal, standard size, narrow size distribution, PPM cleanness, non- magnetic etc.

The specific tungsten carbide we use are the famous brand products. They have coarse crystalline structure with super hardness and wear resistance, high bending strength, thermal and corrosion resistance.

R & D
The procedures are as follows: product or service desired from customer, classification of quality characteristics, design, a prototype, requirement testing, verification and validation, failure mode and reliability, pre-production, lot production.

Structure Inspection
The Optical Microscope Imaging Apparatus (OMIA) is used to precisely measure size and shape of the products. The Scanning Electron Microscope (SEM) is used to probe the microscopic world on the micron structure. The Scanning Acoustic Microscope (SAM) is used to look for the defectives like flaws, delamination and holes hidden inside the products.

Thermal Stability Test
Differential scanning calorimetry -Thermogravimetric - differential thermal analyzer (DSC-DTA ) . When heating PDC cutters in the oxidizing atmosphere, we measure the initial oxidation temperature, and mass loss rate and weight loss ratio to determine the degree of oxidation and heat resistance.

Wear Ratio
Wear Ratio (WR) is a form of expression of PDC cutter abrasion resistance. WR is defined as the ratio of the standard wheel weight loss to the PDC cutter diamond layer weight loss, after it is ground by the wheel. The larger the ratio, the higher the WR, the higher the abrasion resistance. Heat WR is the abrasion resistance after PDC cutter passes through a heat treatment, and it is used to determine the abrasion variation of PDC cutters.

Impact Strength
Impact Strength (IS) is the ability to resist impact fracture of PDC cutters, and we test it by using a method in which an impact load simulating underground drilling hits PDC cutter. The method is to let a ball free fall from a certain height and hit the central area of diamond layer surface repeatedly until there appears any flaw or break in the PDC cutter. Then we count the cumulative impact number; and calculate the impact Joule power per time and the accumulating power value that represents the impact toughness of the PDC cutter.

But PDC cutter receiving the impact load is on the surface edge, instead of the centre in actual working process. So we invent an innovative test method for IS. We let a ball hit the outer circumference of a PDC cutter diamond surface. According to probability statistic law, the impact points of the ball compose an outer circle line. This method most approaches the real condition of hitting PDC cutter.

Application Test
Each Diamond has developed a proprietary technology for application test. We install an engineering drill machine with power measurement on a giant platform. Firstly, make a drill bit using the PDC cutters to be tested. Secondly, drill a big and homogeneous crystal granite with the drill bit. During the drilling process, the bit will undergo, selectively or all, heavy-duty friction abrasion, impact abrasion, erosion and thermal abrasion. Lastly, receive a comprehensive evaluation of the PDC cutter overall performance including durability and drilling rate etc. And the evaluation data can be used to score the validation and verification of the product.

Field Test
Finally, we test PDC cutters in real work environment. Geological formations are so complicated, and there are different choices of drill machines and drilling parameters. So only the test data in site can decide whether the product meets the user requirements or not. This is the final phase for valuing PDC cutters.
