Design Concepts

PDC cutters Structure

Tungsten Carbide Matrix
Tungsten carbide matrix strongly supports diamond surface. We manufacture custom sizes and can provide custom shapes and sizes to meet our clients satisfaction.

Bond Interface
A lot of different bonding-pattern interfaces are formed between the tungsten carbide matrix and the diamond layer. This strengthens their bonding strength. Generally, a larger contact surface with elements evenly distributed, symmetric and forms an circular arc, enhances the bonding strength.

Diamond Surface
Diamond surface should be the hardest and the most abrasive resistant material in the world. The diamond layer thickness varies by requirements and manufacture technology level.

Diamond Layer Chamfer
Diamond layer has to be chamfered accordingly, so PDC cutters can improve cutting ability and durability under different drilling conditions.

Tungsten Carbide Chamfer
The bottom chamfered tungsten carbide enlarges the contact area between PDC cutters and drill bit body. This contact area not only increases the brazing and sintering strength, but also reduces stress concentration.

Diamond Surface Polishing
The diamond surface polishing is a technique used to reduce friction against rock debris. Our highly abrasive resistant PDC cutters make the polishing unnecessary.

PDC Cutter Working Principle

1. In formations where rocks are plastic, when under drilling pressure and torque from PDC cutter diamond edges, rocks crack internally or flow plastically. Then, they break down and becomes debris.

2. In brittle formations, the PDC cutters cause those brittle rocks to rupture. PDC cutters fracture the rock by strain and shear force which results the drills' penetration rate to be higher.

3. In hard and abrasive formations, the flat diamond surfaces of PDC cutters are not strong enough to withstand the pressure and impact. They will start chipping, spalling or delaminating. The dome shaped PDC cutters are used to solve this problem. Their cambered surfaces can help to fracture the rock first. Although dome shaped of PDC cutters can last longer than flat ones, their penetration rate is lower.

Wear Mode

PDC cutters' cutting mechanism is shearing, pressing and scraping. The main characteristics of wear mode are friction wear and fragmentation wear, besides thermal wear by diamond carbonization caused by inadequate cooling or in deep hard formation.

Friction Wear
If PDC cutter cuts a softer formation by virtue of shearing, pressing or scraping the diamond working edge surface will be slightly plastically deformed and cause micro broken. This kind of wear is defined as friction wear (pure wear).

Fragmentation Wear
If a PDC cutter is repeatedly impacted, pressed or scraped; and the heavy load stress is bigger than the deformation limit of the diamond working edge surface, the transverse crack behind the edge surface will occur by a cumulative residual stress, and then the crack propagation causes some local cutting edges separated, resulting in chipping or spalling damage. This kind of wear is defined as fragmentation wear.

Ideal Wear Mode
Ideal Wear Mode is a characteristic of high performance PDC cutters. No matter how it drills the formation that is suitable for PDC cutter working, it exhibits excellent impact ability and high wear resistance for a long time. PDC cutter diamond working edge surface provides micro friction wear, in which it keeps the initial or greater back angle, and the approximate plane or arc back surface all along.

PDC Cutter Failure

The failure modes of PDC cutters are abrasion, fragmentation and delamination. PDC cutter's damage causes the failure of PDC drill bit, but its failures also include tooth column broken and bit body abrasive erosion etc. Under normal circumstances, PDC drill bit fails by the reaction force (moment) of downhole rock and the rock debris abrasion. However, there are other causes of non- normal damage of PDC cutter and PDC drill bit.

1. Gravel
As a PDC drill bit reaches agglomerate rock in gravel formation, the working edge surfaces usually breaks or delaminates.

2. Hard Sandwich and Dip Formation
As a PDC drill bit enters hard sandwich or dip formation from softer one, the drill machine tends to jump back or seize up. Some diamond working edge surfaces of PDC cutter in the bit crown will cause fracture, delamination or column broken due to the extraordinary big impacts.
In addition, the reaction force mostly concentrates on the diamond working edge in hard formation; whereas in soft formation, the force spreads on the whole diamond surface，therefore, PDC cutters are more likely to be damaged in hard formation than in soft one.

3. Improper Parameters
If the specific hydraulic power is too high, the cut rock debris will causes excessively abrasion erosion on the bit body, so that the teeth column or nozzle are easy to fall off. On the contrary, if the specific hydraulic power is too low, PDC drill bits will be balled up, and then reducing cooling and cleaning efficiency, resulting in thermal abrasion on the diamond working edge surface.
