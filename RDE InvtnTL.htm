Innovation Timeline

R&D Team

Our R&D staff constitutes experts from Zhengzhou Research Institute in Abrasives and Grinding field. An engineering executive from White Dove Abrasives Group and a PhD in material science founded Each Diamond Ltd. Our R&D efforts are also supported by partner companies in oil and gas services.

Members of our R&D team have made contributions to technical development of China’s superabrasive industry. Our team inherits tradition of dedication and with their experience, we will keep on innovating.

Timeline

2007 - 2017

Developing and Manufacturing PDCs

2017

  • Successfully developed much higher abrasion resistant PDC cutters.

2016

  • Successfully developed a new kind of diamond wire drawing dies. It improved abrasion resistance.

2015

  • Achieved in increasing of synthesizing pressure at sintering to 1.3 times higher than industrial standard. The performance of PDC cutters was dramatically lifted.

2014

  • Successfully developed a new kind of tungsten carbide for PDC cutter substrate with our supplier. This substrate contains Re elements, and makes the PDC tougher.

2013

  • Enhanced thermal stability PDCs were released.

2012

  • Used water-free electrolyte to remove metallic catalyst.

2011

  • Appied smart sintering at higher HTHP.
  • Applied proprietary process of treatment and coating on super abrasive micron powders.
  • EACH Diamond partnered with an oil and gas services company.
  • EACH Diamond Ltd. was founded.

2010

  • Cconducted PDC thermal stability test.
  • Built a drilling test platform to simulate real world drilling in order to realistically test PDC cutters' properties.

2009

  • Developed reduction treatment process for diamond micron powder.
  • Used scanning electronic microscope (SEM) and raman spectrum to study bonding in diamond.

2008

  • Developed a suite of three high temperature metal cups to protect sintering.
  • Used scanning acoustic microscope (SAM) to detect defects in diamond.

2007

  • Applied salt tubes and salt caps in cell sealing

2005 - 2006

Developing and Producing Micron Diamond Powder for PDC cutters

  • Conducted ICP-AES test to control surface cleanness.
  • Used laser particle analyzer to analyze and control particle size distribution.
  • Developed a physical and chemical purification process to extract impurities from diamond particles externally as well as internally.
  • Produced diamond micron powder with a large and unique jet mill. The powder were chunky crystalline particles with integrity shapes.
