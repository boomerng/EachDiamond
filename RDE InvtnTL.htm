Innovation Timeline

Background

Since General Electric (GE) invented synthetic diamond in 1955, material processing has undergone revolutionary changes, bringing us new materials.

At the beginning of 1960’s, three institutes under the then Ministry of Machinery Industry of China: Zhengzhou Research Institute for Abrasives and Grinding, Jinan Foundry and Metalforming Machinery Research Institute, and Hefei General Machinery Research Institute started joint research on synthetic diamonds. In 1963, they made China’s first synthetic diamonds. In 1967, Cubic Boron Nitride (CBN) was first produced. In 1974, Xi’an Branch of China Coal Research Institute successfully developed synthetic diamond drill bits, ushering in a new era of industrial application of synthetic diamonds in China.

In 1996, Baoding BADA and WINTER set up a joint venture. Despite its failure in business, it has remarkably improved the quality of China’s synthetic monocrystalline diamonds. Since then, Zhongnan Diamond Co., Ltd. and Huanghe Whirlwind Co., Ltd. have dominated the global market. The production output of synthetic diamond in China now accounts for 90% of the world market.

From 1999 to 2007, China’s renowned producers overcame technical bottlenecks in making multicrystalline diamonds, CBN, micron diamond powders, and micron CBN powders. They developed a wider range of products and began wholesale industrial application. Their products rapidly dominated the international market.

In 2012, China’s polycrystalline diamond compacts (PDC) for drilling were already of the highest quality in the world. They took the place of imported PDCs in the domestic market and entered the world market.

Our R&D efforts are also strategically supported by an oil and gas services company.

Seasoned members of our R&D team have contributed to the technical development of China’s superabrasive industry. The current team who is very intelligent inherits their tradition of dedication and innovation, and draws on their experiences.

Timeline

2007 - 2017

Developing and Producing PDCs

2017

  • Successfully developed high endurance PDC cutters.

2016

  • The successful development of a new kind of diamond wire drawing die blanks. It provides extremely hardness for abrasion.

2015

  • Pressure of synthesizing at sintering reaches 1.3 times that of industrial standard, and the performance of PDC cutters is dramatically lifted.

2014

  • One kind of tungsten carbide for PDC cutter substrate is successfully developed with the stragetic supplier. This substrate that contains Re elements makes the PDC more tough.

2013

  • Enhanced Thermal Stability PDCs are released.

2012

  • Non-water electrolyte is used to remove metallic catalysts.

2011

  • EACH Diamond Ltd. is founded.
  • EACH Diamond is strategically allied with an oil and gas services company.
  • The proprietary process of treatment and coating on super abrasive micron powders is applied.
  • The smart sintering at higher HTHP is applied.

2010

  • PDC Thermal Stability test is conducted.
  • A drilling test platform is built; Impact Toughness and Wear Resistance for PDC are tested with a way to stimulate load more realistically.

2009

  • The diamond micron powders are treated by reducing process.
  • The Scanning Electronic Microscope (SEM) and Raman Spectrum are used to study bonding diamond to diamond.

2008

  • The suite of three high temperature metal cups is applied for protecting sintering.
  • The Scanning Acoustic Microscope (SAM) is used to detect defectives.

2007

  • Applying salt tubes and salt caps for cell sealing

2005 - 2006

Developing and Producing Micron Diamond Powder for PDCs

  • The diamond micron powders are made by a large and special jet mill, getting blocky crystalline particles with integrity shapes.
  • The internal and external impurities of diamond particles are removed with the physical chemical purification technology.
  • The laser particle size analyzer is used to analyze and control particle size distribution.
  • ICP-AES test is conducted to control surface cleanness.
